Beginning in March 2023 and increasing in intensity around June, Canada has been affected by an ongoing record-setting series of wildfires. These have affected all Canadian provinces and territories except Nunavut. The 2023 wildfire season is the worst in Canada's modern history.

As of June 5, 2,214 fires have burned 43,000 square kilometres (10,600,000 acres). As of June 6, there were 413 active wildfires, 249 of which were deemed "out of control".

Wildfires have caused air quality alerts, evacuations, and a large wildfire smoke spread reaching across North America and Europe.
